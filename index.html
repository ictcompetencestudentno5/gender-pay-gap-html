About the Gender Pay Gap

Understanding wage inequality and its effects on Filipino workers

What is Gender Pay Gap?

The gender pay gap refers to the difference in average earnings between men and women. In the Philippines, progress has been made toward gender equality, yet wage inequality still exists across many sectors.

Women working full-time make

81%

of what men earn.

Behind this number are millions of hardworking Filipino women.

  • The Philippines ranks 20th out of 148 countries in gender equality (World Economic Forum, 2025).
  • Men earn about 15.7% to 16.2% more than women on average (ILO).
  • In the agriculture sector, men earn ₱310.16/day while women earn ₱285.51/day (PIDS).
  • Among college graduates, the pay gap narrows to around 12% (PNA).

The Main Causes of the Pay Gap

  • Occupational segregation — women often work in lower-paying sectors.
  • Undervaluation of work typically done by women.
  • Lack of opportunities in leadership and STEM fields.
  • Discrimination and cultural expectations.

How to Close the Gap

Experts suggest the following steps to reduce the gender pay gap:

  • Implement stronger laws ensuring equal pay for equal work.
  • Encourage transparency in salary reporting.
  • Support women’s education and participation in high-paying fields.
  • Provide flexible working arrangements and childcare support.

Systemic Racism & Pay Inequality

The history of gender and racial wage gaps is deeply connected to the broader history of labor in America. From the denial of wages to Black women during slavery and its aftermath, to the lasting inequalities in health, education, and opportunity faced by Native women due to land dispossession, and to the legal and cultural barriers that limited women’s ability to earn a fair income—our nation’s past reveals a long legacy of discrimination and its enduring impact.
